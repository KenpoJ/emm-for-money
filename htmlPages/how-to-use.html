How the Jar and EMM System Works

You may be familiar with the JARS System of money management taught by T Harv Ecker as part of his Millionaire Mind program. The system is simple and straight-forward. The idea is to separate your net income into six “jars,” or accounts. The money in each jar is committed to a specific purpose or goal.

The Jars

The six jars are these:

Necessities
NEC

50% of your income goes into this jar. Use the money here to pay for all necessities; rent/mortgage, bills, gas, food, etc.

Financial Freedom Account
FFA

10% of your income goes into this jar. This account is used for investment purposes. This will become your “golden goose,” providing a residual income in later years. One rule with this account: Don’t touch it! Let it grow.

Long Term Savings for Spending
LTSS

10% of your income goes into this jar. This account is used to build up a savings for several months and then purchase a “big ticket” item.

Giving
GIVE

10% of your income goes into this jar. It is a good practice to be generous with what you have been given. Donate this portion of your income to a charity. Whether it is a church organization, the local food bank or another charity that helps those less fortunate doesn’t matter. The important thing is to share your wealth.

Education
EDU

10% of your income goes into this jar. Education is important for each of us. This account is used for expenses that will help you develop yourself. Books, mentoring, courses, seminars, etc.

Play
PLAY

10% of your income goes into this jar. This jar is just for what it says. Blow it each month on something fun. If you want you can let it build up for a couple of months to spend all of it on something a little larger. Movies, sports events, concerts, eating out, etc.

My Experience with the Jar System

When I first learned about the JARS System I wanted to try it immediately. However, when I looked at the amount of money I was bringing in and compared it to the percentages that I was supposed to put into each jar, that wasn’t happening. Our expenses were far more than 50% of my net income. So I had to modify the percentages, and the percentages would change with each paycheck because of different bills that were due at each time during the month.

So, once all my expenses were added up I would put that much into my necessities jar, plus a few dollars extra. At the beginning it was sometimes 65 - 70 percent. It didn’t leave much, but the rest of the money I divided into the other jars. In the beginning it may have only been 20 or 30 dollars, but the important thing is to do it. As time goes on the amounts will grow, either by your income increasing or your ability to simplify your lives, and thus your expenses.

EMM: Effortless Money Management

In the basic free version of this tool you will be able to tell us your income, list your expenses and set up an allowance for you and your spouse. the tool will then make the calculations necessary to distribute your money into each jar, giving you the percentage and the dollar amount.

The advanced version, available with a small monthly subscription, will allow you to add a Debt Clearance Account JAR, helping you to become free of debt quickly and easily. It will also keep track of the numbers, how much money has been put into each jar over different time periods, your income and cost of expenses.
